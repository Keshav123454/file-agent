The cow is a gentle and useful animal found in many parts of the world.
It is known for its calm nature and strong connection with humans.
In rural areas, cows are considered an important part of daily life.
They provide milk, which is a nutritious and essential food.
Milk from cows is used to make butter, cheese, and yogurt.
The cow is a herbivorous animal that eats grass and fodder.
It spends most of its day grazing in fields.
A cow has a large body with four legs and a long tail.
Its tail helps it drive away flies and insects.
The cow has two horns on its head, though some may not.
It has big, expressive eyes and a peaceful appearance.
Cows are domesticated animals that live with humans.
Farmers take care of cows by feeding and protecting them.
In many cultures, cows are treated with great respect.
In India, the cow is considered a sacred animal.
It is often associated with purity and motherhood.
Cow dung is also useful and used as manure or fuel.
It helps in improving soil fertility in agriculture.
Cow urine is sometimes used in traditional practices.
The cow gives birth to calves, which grow into adult cattle.
A baby cow is called a calf.
Cows communicate through sounds like mooing.
